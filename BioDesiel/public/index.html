What is Biodiesel?

Biodiesel is a renewable fuel made from vegetable oils, animal fats, or recycled grease. It's produced through a process called transesterification, which converts triglycerides into fatty acid methyl esters.

  • ✓ Reduces greenhouse gas emissions by up to 75%
  • ✓ Compatible with existing diesel engines
  • ✓ Biodegradable and non-toxic
  • ✓ Improves engine lubrication

Other Alternative Energy Sources

While biodiesel powers transport and machinery, building a green grid relies on a variety of alternative energy systems.

Solar Energy

Harvests sunlight via photovoltaic panels to generate pure electricity, fueling residential grids and commercial power hubs.

Wind Energy

Deploys massive kinetic wind turbines both onshore and offshore to safely convert air currents into mechanical grid power.

Hydroelectric

Utilizes flowing water through controlled dams or rivers to spin fast turbines, delivering consistent, reliable energy.

Geothermal

Taps into natural deep underground thermal pockets to produce clean steam, heating systems, and continuous baseline power.
